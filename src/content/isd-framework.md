# ISD Coach: Capability & Priority Roadmap

| Capability | Description | V1 Priority |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Explain ISD Guardrails in Plain Language** | Explains what each step requires, why it matters, and the risk of skipping or compressing it, using the ISD skill as the primary source. | **MUST** — Core Function |
| **Suggest the Next Best Step** | Provides situational guidance by recommending the most logical next action within the ISD flow based on user descriptions. | **MUST** — Situational Guidance |
| **Surface Required Deliverables** | Provides clarity by defining what is expected of a specific role at a given step so they know what "done" looks like. | **MUST** — Clarity Function |
| **Evaluate Readiness to Progress** | Serves as a quality gate, helping users assess if they are ready to move to the next phase using Quality Standards as a reference. | **MUST** — Quality Gate |
| **Route to the Correct Framework** | Identifies which of the four supporting frameworks applies to a situation and guides the user accordingly. | **MUST** — Framework Navigation |
| **Ask Clarifying Questions** | Ensures precision by asking targeted questions for ambiguous prompts rather than providing generic responses. | **MUST** — Precision Input |
| **Recommend Relevant Tools** | Offers practical support by mentioning tools like **TheyDo**, **FigJam**, and **Figma** in context (not as a tutorial). | **SHOULD** — Practical Support |
| **Provide Decision Support** | Integrates the Decision Making Framework to help users navigate escalations and determine who decides what. | **SHOULD** — Decision Making Integration |
| **Summarize Good Collaboration** | Integrates the Collaboration Framework to explain who to involve and when for key rituals. | **SHOULD** — Collaboration Integration |
| **Provide Argumentation Support** | Supplies practitioners with business-grounded arguments (risk, cost, impact) to defend UX work when it is deprioritized or challenged. | **SHOULD** — Advocacy Function |
| **Mindset-Aware Guidance** | Connects outputs to the five **Digital Travel Mindsets** (Risk Mitigation, Comfort Zone Cruising, etc.) and corresponding **DX lenses** (Sees you / Guides you / Celebrates you / Fallback). | **SHOULD** — Strategic Depth |

## Phase 1: Empathize

| Category | Understand Users & Context | Identify Service Ecosystem | Stakeholder Analysis & Relations |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **What** | Deep dive into user needs, behaviors, and motivations within their specific context. | Mapping the broader system of actors, touchpoints, and relationships involved. | Analyzing the impact, interest, and influence of internal and external stakeholders. |
| **Why** | To build a solution grounded in real human needs rather than assumptions. | To see the "big picture" and ensure a holistic approach to service delivery. | To align goals, manage expectations, and gain early project buy-in. |
| **Service Designer / Delivery Manager** | Defines research scope, objectives, and ensures business alignment. | Visualizes the end-to-end service landscape and identifies silos. | Facilitates stakeholder alignment workshops and identifies project owners. |
| **User Researcher** | Leads qualitative research, interviews, and ethnographic studies. | Identifies user-side pain points within the existing ecosystem. | Gathers functional and non-functional requirements from stakeholders. |
| **CX Designer** | Creates initial empathy maps and drafts persona archetypes. | Maps high-level user journeys across the existing ecosystem. | Maps how stakeholder decisions influence the final customer experience. |
| **Conversational Designer** | Audits existing conversational patterns and identifies AI/automation opportunities. | Defines conversational touchpoints within the broader service map. | Aligns conversational objectives with technical and business stakeholders. |
| **CX Writer** | Audits existing content, documentation, and established brand tone. | Maps the flow of information and messaging across the ecosystem. | Interviews stakeholders to define communication constraints and goals. |
| **UI Designer** | Researches visual trends and ensures accessibility standards are met early. | Reviews existing UI components and design systems in use. | Gathers technical and visual brand constraints from key stakeholders. |
| **CX Analysis** | Gap Analysis; Persona Development; Empathic Mapping. | System Mapping; Touchpoint Audit. | Power/Interest Grid; Stakeholder Interviews. |
| **CX Design** | Contextual Inquiry; User Stories. | Service Blueprinting (Current State). | Relationship Mapping. |
| **CX Testing** | Heuristic Evaluation; Existing Service Audit. | Traceability Matrix. | Stakeholder Validation Sessions. |
| **Measurements** | Research Participation Rates; Qualitative Insight Volume. | Ecosystem Complexity Score. | [Stakeholder Buy-in / NPS](https://www.figma.com/design/TVA7IAa47KrtogLDGQwXps/CoP-DX---Design-Patterns?node-id=304-1315) |

## Phase 2: Define

| Category | Problem Definition & Visioning | Needs & Goals Alignment | Opportunity Prioritization |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **What** | Synthesizing research to create a clear problem statement and future vision. | Ensuring user needs align with business objectives and technical constraints. | Evaluating and ranking potential solutions based on impact and feasibility. |
| **Why** | To ensure the team is solving the right problem with a unified direction. | To avoid "feature creep" and ensure the solution is viable and valuable. | To focus resources on high-value initiatives that deliver the most ROI. |
| **Service Designer / Delivery Manager** | Facilitates visioning sessions and defines Key Performance Indicators (KPIs). | Balances user needs with business goals and strategic roadmaps. | Leads prioritization frameworks (e.g., MoSCoW) with stakeholders. |
| **User Researcher** | Synthesizes research findings into actionable insights and "How Might We" statements. | Validates that the proposed vision actually addresses identified user needs. | Provides evidence-based recommendations for feature prioritization. |
| **CX Designer** | Refines personas and develops the future-state user journey vision. | Ensures the journey aligns with both user goals and brand objectives. | Identifies "low-hanging fruit" and high-impact CX improvements. |
| **Conversational Designer** | Defines the "Personality" and "Voice" of the AI/conversational interface. | Aligns conversational goals with user intent and technical capabilities. | Prioritizes conversational use cases based on user frequency and complexity. |
| **CX Writer** | Defines the content strategy and editorial guidelines for the solution. | Ensures messaging aligns with user literacy levels and business tone. | Prioritizes critical content updates and information architecture. |
| **UI Designer** | Establishes the visual design direction and mood boards. | Ensures visual goals align with accessibility and platform constraints. | Prioritizes the design of core UI components and layouts. |
| **CX Analysis** | Root Cause Analysis; Vision Mapping. | Goal Alignment Matrix; SWOT Analysis. | Impact vs. Effort Analysis. |
| **CX Design** | Future State Journey Mapping. | User Story Mapping. | Feature Roadmapping. |
| **CX Testing** | Concept Validation. | Desirability Testing. | Feasibility Review. |
| **Measurements** | Problem Statement Clarity. | Business-User Alignment Score. | Priority Confidence Rating. |

## Phase 3: Ideate

| Category | Idea Generation | Concept Development | Feasibility & Risk Assessment |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **What** | Brainstorming a wide range of creative solutions without constraints. | Fleshing out the best ideas into more detailed and tangible concepts. | Evaluating concepts against technical, legal, and operational constraints. |
| **Why** | To foster innovation and explore unconventional ways to solve problems. | To visualize how ideas would work in practice and gather early feedback. | To identify potential roadblocks and risks before investing in prototyping. |
| **Service Designer / Delivery Manager** | Facilitates brainstorming workshops using diverse creative techniques. | Coordinates the development of multi-faceted service concepts. | Works with technical leads to assess operational and cost feasibility. |
| **User Researcher** | Encourages "blue-sky" thinking based on latent user needs. | Evaluates concepts through the lens of user desirability and mental models. | Identifies potential user-related risks or ethical concerns (e.g., privacy). |
| **CX Designer** | Sketches diverse experience concepts and interaction models. | Develops storyboards and high-level wireframes for selected concepts. | Assesses the impact of concepts on the broader customer journey. |
| **Conversational Designer** | Ideates different conversational flows and interaction styles. | Drafts sample dialogues and interaction scripts for key scenarios. | Evaluates the feasibility of AI models and Natural Language Processing (NLP). |
| **CX Writer** | Brainstorms catchy headlines, clear labels, and engaging microcopy. | Develops content outlines and information hierarchy for concepts. | Evaluates the legal and compliance implications of the proposed messaging. |
| **UI Designer** | Explores various visual styles, layouts, and interactive patterns. | Creates "Lo-Fi" prototypes and visual mockups of the concepts. | Assesses the feasibility of visual designs across different devices/platforms. |
| **CX Analysis** | Brainstorming; SCAMPER. | Concept Sketching. | Risk Matrix. |
| **CX Design** | Storyboarding. | Lo-Fi Wireframing. | Prototyping (Low Fidelity). |
| **CX Testing** | Dot Voting; Feedback Sessions. | Guerrilla Testing. | Technical Feasibility Review. |
| **Measurements** | Idea Volume/Diversity. | Concept Desirability Score. | Risk Mitigation Confidence. |

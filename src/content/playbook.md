# UX Process Playbook

## Overview
This playbook provides four distinct workflow variants derived from the full **Infused Service Design framework**, adapted for specific product contexts and scopes [cite: 741, 742, 818].

### The Four Workflows
* **Workflow 1: Large Features (Full Discovery-to-Delivery):** A complete process for significant product initiatives [cite: 820].
* **Workflow 2: Small Improvements (Lean Validation):** A streamlined approach for incremental enhancements [cite: 821].
* **Workflow 3: Exploratory Innovation (Open-Ended Research):** Discovery-focused work without immediate delivery [cite: 822].
* **Workflow 4: Maintenance Work (Optimization Cycles):** Continuous improvement of existing features [cite: 823].

---

## When to Adapt vs. Follow Rigorously

| Follow Rigorously When [cite: 825] | Adapt the Process When [cite: 830] |
| :--- | :--- |
| High business impact or risk [cite: 826] | Time constraints are severe (document what is skipped) [cite: 831] |
| Multiple teams/stakeholders involved [cite: 827] | Low-risk, reversible changes [cite: 832] |
| New product areas or user segments [cite: 828] | Well-understood problem space [cite: 833] |
| Regulatory or compliance requirements [cite: 828] | Small user impact [cite: 834] |
| Significant technical complexity [cite: 828] | Rapid experimentation required [cite: 835] |
| Budget >€50k or timeline >3 months [cite: 829] | Resource constraints (e.g., no researcher) [cite: 836] |

> **Golden Rule:** Never skip understanding the problem. You can compress how you understand it, but never bypass it entirely [cite: 837].

## Workflow 1: Large Features (Full Discovery-to-Delivery)
**When to use:** New features, major redesigns, cross-journey initiatives, or strategic bets [cite: 844].
**Timeline:** 3–6 months [cite: 845].
**Team:** Full cross-functional team (Service Designer, Researcher, UX/UI Designers, UX Writer, PO, Engineers) [cite: 846, 847].

### Phase 1: UNDERSTAND — Empathize (Weeks 1–6) [cite: 852]
* **Step 1: Stakeholder Alignment (Week 1):** Map ecosystem, facilitate alignment workshops, and identify target users/mindsets [cite: 854, 856, 857, 862].
* **Step 2: Data & Insights Gathering (Weeks 1–2):** Analyze existing qualitative/quantitative data, conduct competitive benchmarking, and identify knowledge gaps [cite: 873, 875, 876, 878].
* **Step 3: User Research & Problem Exploration (Weeks 2–5):** Conduct primary research (interviews, observations), map findings to journeys, and validate hypotheses [cite: 890, 892, 893, 894].

### Phase 2: DEFINE (Weeks 7–9) [cite: 907]
* **Step 4: Problem Framing & Root-Cause Validation (Weeks 7–8):** Distinguish symptoms from causes, quantify significance, and scope problems to journey moments [cite: 909, 911, 912].
* **Step 5: Goals & Success Metrics (Week 8):** Define experience objectives and establish HEART framework success criteria [cite: 925, 927, 928].
* **Step 6: Opportunity Prioritization (Week 9):** Rank problems by criticality and create a sequenced design roadmap [cite: 942, 944, 947].

### Phase 3: EXPLORE — Ideate & Prototype (Weeks 10–16) [cite: 962, 1016]
* **Step 7: Solution Generation:** Create conceptual directions, user flows, and interaction models [cite: 964, 966, 967].
* **Step 8: Co-Creation:** Validate concepts with stakeholders/users and test information architecture [cite: 980, 982].
* **Step 9: Feasibility & Risk Check:** Assess technical feasibility with engineers and develop service blueprints [cite: 999, 1001, 1002].
* **Step 10 & 11: Prototyping & User Testing:** Translate concepts into testable prototypes, conduct usability testing, and iterate designs based on behavior [cite: 1022, 1024, 1038, 1040, 1043].

### Phase 4: BUILD — Implement (Weeks 17–24+) [cite: 1060]
* **Step 12: Dev Handover:** Package final designs with logic/specs and review implementations in progress [cite: 1062, 1064, 1067].
* **Step 13: Delivery & Release:** Validate designs in staging, verify accessibility (WCAG), and conduct final visual QA [cite: 1077, 1083, 1085, 1088].

### Phase 5: LISTEN — Post-Launch (Ongoing) [cite: 1097]
* **Step 14: Evaluate Performance:** Track KPIs and HEART metrics (Weeks 1–4 post-launch) [cite: 1099, 1101].
* **Step 15: Continuous Improvement:** Analyze data for optimization and update the design system [cite: 1120, 1122, 1124].

## Workflow 2: Small Improvements (Lean Validation)
**When to use:** Bug fixes with UX implications, minor tweaks, or single-screen optimizations [cite: 1140].
**Timeline:** 2–4 weeks [cite: 1141].

1.  **Rapid Understand (Days 1–3):** Quick stakeholder sync and review of existing analytics/support tickets [cite: 1150, 1153, 1154].
2.  **Quick Define (Days 4–5):** Frame the problem, define success metrics, and check technical feasibility [cite: 1167, 1174, 1175, 1177].
3.  **Design & Test (Week 1):** Design 1–2 options and perform quick user validation (guerrilla or unmoderated testing) [cite: 1186, 1189, 1191].
4.  **Build & Validate (Weeks 2–3):** Support dev, review in staging, and monitor metrics immediately post-launch [cite: 1209, 1212, 1213, 1214].

## Workflow 3: Exploratory Innovation (Open-Ended Research)
**When to use:** Future-facing research, new market exploration, or discovery-focused innovation sprints [cite: 1224].
**Timeline:** 4–8 weeks (no immediate build) [cite: 1225].

* **Frame Exploration (Week 1):** Define broad research questions and target users [cite: 1234, 1237, 1238].
* **Deep Research (Weeks 2–6):** Conduct generative research (ethnography, contextual inquiry) to uncover unmet needs [cite: 1248, 1251].
* **Synthesis & Opportunity Framing (Weeks 7–8):** Transform data into actionable insights and future-state journey maps [cite: 1269, 1272, 1274].
* **Socialize & Roadmap (Week 9):** Present findings to leadership and integrate opportunities into product strategy [cite: 1286, 1293, 1295].

---

## Workflow 4: Maintenance Work (Optimization Cycles)
**When to use:** Conversion improvements, usability refinements, or analytics-driven enhancements [cite: 1311].
**Timeline:** 2–3 weeks per cycle (recurring) [cite: 1312].

1.  **Identify & Analyze (Days 1–5):** Review funnel drop-offs, errors, and heuristic evaluations [cite: 1320, 1323, 1325].
2.  **Hypothesize & Design (Week 1):** Form a testable hypothesis and design alternative variants [cite: 1338, 1341, 1342].
3.  **Test & Iterate (Week 2):** Run A/B tests or unmoderated testing to validate improvements [cite: 1359, 1362, 1364].
4.  **Implement & Measure (Week 3+):** Deploy the winning variant and confirm impact via performance reports [cite: 1377, 1384, 1395].

## Decision Framework: Choosing the Right Workflow
Answer these questions to select a workflow [cite: 1399, 1401]:
1.  **New vs. Existing?** [cite: 1402]
    * New → Proceed to Q2.
    * Existing → Proceed to Q4.
2.  **Is the problem well-understood?** [cite: 1404]
    * No → **Workflow 3 (Exploratory Innovation)**.
    * Yes → Proceed to Q3.
3.  **Scope & Risk?** [cite: 1406, 1412]
    * Multiple teams / high effort (>4 weeks dev) → **Workflow 1 (Large Features)**.
    * Single team / low effort (<2 weeks dev) → **Workflow 2 (Small Improvements)**.
4.  **Driver for existing change?** [cite: 1415]
    * Analytics friction → **Workflow 4 (Maintenance Work)**.
    * Support tickets / minor issue → **Workflow 2 (Small Improvements)**.
    * Strategic enhancement → **Workflow 1 (Large Features)**.

## Quality Gates: When to STOP [cite: 1459, 1465]
Pause and reassess if:
* You cannot articulate the problem clearly [cite: 1466].
* Stakeholders fundamentally disagree on goals [cite: 1467].
* There is no way to measure success [cite: 1468].
* Technical feasibility is unknown and risky [cite: 1469].
* Major accessibility concerns are identified late [cite: 1470].

---

## Communication Cadence [cite: 1481]
* **Large Features:** Weekly or bi-weekly structured updates with phase gates [cite: 1482, 1483, 1484].
* **Small Improvements:** Milestone-based updates (kickoff, review, launch) [cite: 1486, 1487].
* **Exploratory Innovation:** Bi-weekly previews with a "big reveal" at the end [cite: 1489, 1494, 1495].
* **Maintenance:** Monthly rollup of optimizations via a metrics dashboard [cite: 1496, 1497, 1498].
